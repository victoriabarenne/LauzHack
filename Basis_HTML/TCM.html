Compare different models

Here, you have the possibility to change the values of different parameters affecting the spread, and to compare the results. These simulations were done with a total population of a million, divided into smaller communities of 100 individuals.

You can play around with the following parameters:

  • μG, the average global infections caused by a single infected individual,
  • μL, the average inter-communal infections caused by a single infected individual within this community,
  • D, the average duration of the infection, during which individuals are contagious.

A smaller μG (resp. μL) represents less frequent interactions on a global (resp. local) level. Thus, changing these parameters will let you represent different degrees of confinement or behavior during confinement.

Note that: μG and μL are the average direct infections. Note the whole chain of infections it will then cause (à reformuler, mais en gros préciser que c’est juste la première génération d’infection)
